# Food store

A good food store is built from things you already eat, that keep without a fridge,
and that you rotate so nothing is wasted. Aim for a few weeks of meals to start, then
grow it. Favour calorie-dense, low-fuel foods.

> **Tip:** Store what you eat and eat what you store — use the oldest first and
> replace it. A store you never touch goes off and surprises you when you need it.

## Staples that keep

- [ ] Rice, pasta, or other grains
- [ ] Dried beans, lentils, and split peas
- [ ] Flour, oats, and a little salt and sugar
- [ ] Cooking oil
- [ ] Tinned vegetables, beans, and fruit

## Ready-to-eat and quick energy

- [ ] Foods that need no cooking (nuts, dried fruit, bars)
- [ ] Spreads (nut butter), crackers
- [ ] Tea, coffee, or other comforts

## Flavour and nutrition

- [ ] Salt, spices, stock, and herbs
- [ ] A multivitamin if your store is limited
- [ ] Foods for any special diets or allergies in the household

## Cooking and storage

- [ ] A stove or means to cook off-grid, and fuel
- [ ] Manual can opener
- [ ] Airtight, pest-proof containers
- [ ] Labels with dates; rotate the oldest first

## For longer term

- [ ] Seeds for a garden (see seed-saving and crop guides)
- [ ] A way to preserve a harvest (drying, fermenting, pickling)

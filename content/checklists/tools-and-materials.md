# Tools and materials

When you can't call a tradesperson or pop to a shop, a modest set of hand tools and
materials lets you repair, build, and adapt. Favour hand tools that need no power,
and keep a stock of the fixings and materials that jobs always seem to need.

> **Tip:** Hand tools that work without electricity are worth their space. Keep them
> sharp and dry — see the *sharpen and repair hand tools* and *make your own hand
> tools* guides.

## Cutting and shaping

- [ ] Knife and a sharpening stone
- [ ] Hand saw (wood) and a hacksaw (metal)
- [ ] Axe or hatchet
- [ ] Files and a chisel

## Driving and fixing

- [ ] Hammer and a mallet
- [ ] Screwdrivers (assorted) or a multi-bit driver
- [ ] Pliers and an adjustable spanner
- [ ] Hand drill and bits

## Measuring and marking

- [ ] Tape measure and a ruler
- [ ] Spirit level
- [ ] Pencil and chalk line

## Fixings and materials

- [ ] Assorted nails, screws, and bolts
- [ ] Strong cord, rope, and wire
- [ ] Duct tape and other tapes
- [ ] Glue and sealant
- [ ] Tarpaulin and plastic sheeting
- [ ] Spare timber and offcuts

## Safety and care

- [ ] Work gloves and eye protection
- [ ] Dust mask
- [ ] Oil or grease to protect metal from rust
- [ ] A dry box or roll to keep tools in

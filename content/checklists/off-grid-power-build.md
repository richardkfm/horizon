# Off-grid power build

What you need on hand before you start the *set up off-grid power* plan:
sizing your load, wiring solar, and storing it safely in a battery bank.
Gather this before you buy panels so you don't pay for capacity you don't need.

> **Spec:** Confirm your numbers with the *how big a solar + battery system do
> you need?* guide before buying anything — sizing mistakes are the most
> expensive ones.

## Sizing and planning

- [ ] List of appliances and their wattage, with hours of daily use
- [ ] Notepad or spreadsheet to total daily watt-hours
- [ ] A clear, unshaded site for panels, ideally facing the sun's best angle

## Solar components

- [ ] Solar panel(s) sized to your load
- [ ] Charge controller (PWM or MPPT) rated for your panel and battery
- [ ] Mounting frame or brackets, angled and weatherproof
- [ ] Weatherproof cable and connectors rated for outdoor use

## Battery storage

- [ ] Battery bank sized to your load (see *store and manage your power*)
- [ ] Battery box or enclosure, ventilated and out of direct sun
- [ ] Inverter if you need standard AC sockets
- [ ] Fuses or circuit breakers sized to your wiring

## Tools

- [ ] Wire strippers and crimping tool
- [ ] Multimeter to check voltage and continuity
- [ ] Screwdrivers and an adjustable spanner
- [ ] Cable ties and conduit to keep runs tidy and protected

## Safety

- [ ] Insulated gloves for working on the battery bank
- [ ] Safety glasses
- [ ] Fire extinguisher rated for electrical fires, kept nearby
- [ ] Manufacturer manuals for panels, controller, and battery kept on hand

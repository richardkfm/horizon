# Water store

Water is the first thing you run short of and the hardest to do without. Aim to
store at least **3 litres per person per day** (drinking and basic hygiene) for a
minimum of a few days — more if you can. Rotate stored water every six months.

> **Tip:** Don't only *store* water — keep the means to *treat* more, so you're not
> limited to what's in the containers. See the water guides on testing and
> treatment.

## Stored water

- [ ] 3+ litres per person per day, for at least 3 days (ideally 2 weeks)
- [ ] Clean, food-grade containers with tight lids
- [ ] Containers dated and rotated every 6 months
- [ ] A small, separate supply in your go-bag

## Treating more water

- [ ] Filter (gravity, pump, or straw type)
- [ ] Purification tablets or unscented household bleach (and a dropper)
- [ ] A pot for boiling and the fuel to do it
- [ ] Clean cloth for pre-filtering cloudy water

## Collecting and carrying

- [ ] Buckets or jerry cans for fetching
- [ ] Clean sheeting or barrels for rainwater catchment
- [ ] Funnel and spare lids

## Hygiene with limited water

- [ ] Hand sanitiser and wet wipes
- [ ] A way to capture and reuse grey water for flushing

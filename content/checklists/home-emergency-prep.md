# Home emergency preparedness

A go-bag is for leaving; this is for staying. Keep your home itself ready to
ride out a blackout, storm, flood, or a medical emergency without a shop trip.
Check it twice a year alongside the go-bag.

> **Tip:** Most items here back a specific guide — water and power outages,
> storms, floods, and the first-aid guides under *Health*. Read the relevant
> guide before you need it, not during.

## Power and light

- [ ] Torches in every room and spare batteries
- [ ] Wind-up or battery radio for emergency broadcasts
- [ ] Power bank, charged, with charging cables
- [ ] Candles and matches kept well away from anything flammable
- [ ] A plan for what stays off during a long blackout (see *prepare for and
      live through a long blackout*)

## Water and food at home

- [ ] Stored drinking water (see the water-store checklist)
- [ ] A few days of food that needs no cooking or refrigeration
- [ ] Manual can opener

## Storm, flood, and fire readiness

- [ ] Sandbags or flood barriers if you're in a flood-prone area
- [ ] Tarpaulin, duct tape, and rope to cover storm damage
- [ ] Fire extinguisher or fire blanket, checked and in date
- [ ] Smoke and carbon monoxide alarms tested
- [ ] Important furniture and documents raised off the floor in flood-prone
      homes

## First aid and medicine

- [ ] First-aid kit, fully stocked (see the first-aid-kit checklist)
- [ ] A few days' supply of regular medicines for everyone in the household
- [ ] A printed copy of basic first-aid steps (bleeding, burns, choking,
      fractures) in case phones are down

## Information and contacts

- [ ] Emergency contact numbers written down, not just saved on a phone
- [ ] A household meeting point if you have to leave separately
- [ ] Copies of insurance and ID documents stored safely, not only digitally
- [ ] Neighbours' contact details for checking on each other

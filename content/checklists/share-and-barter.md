# Goods to share and barter

When money is scarce or shops are shut, communities get by through **mutual aid** —
sharing what they have and trading fairly for what they don't. A small stock of
useful, hard-to-improvise items helps you support neighbours and exchange for things
you need, without exploiting anyone's hardship.

> **Note:** This is about cooperation, not profiteering. Trade fairly, give where you
> can, and never use scarcity to take advantage of people in need — that's a core
> horizon value. See the *make fair group decisions* guide.

## Small, useful, hard to make

- [ ] Matches and lighters
- [ ] Candles and batteries
- [ ] Soap and hygiene items
- [ ] Salt, spices, and seasonings
- [ ] Needles, thread, and safety pins
- [ ] String, cord, and tape

## Health and comfort

- [ ] Basic first-aid supplies (plasters, bandages)
- [ ] Common pain relief and rehydration salts
- [ ] Toothbrushes and toothpaste
- [ ] Warm socks and blankets

## Practical extras

- [ ] Seeds and gardening supplies
- [ ] Hand-tool spares and fixings
- [ ] Water purification tablets
- [ ] Fuel for stoves and lamps

## Skills and knowledge to share

- [ ] First aid and care
- [ ] Repairing tools, clothes, and bikes
- [ ] Growing, cooking, and preserving food
- [ ] Reading, writing, and translation (see the language guides)

## How to share and trade well

- [ ] Agree exchanges openly and fairly, by mutual consent
- [ ] Keep a simple, transparent record if a group pools resources
- [ ] Prioritise the vulnerable; give freely where there is real need
- [ ] Build trust — reliable, honest exchange is worth more than any single item

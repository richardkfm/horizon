# Go-bag (grab-and-go kit)

A go-bag is a single bag, kept packed and by the door, holding what you need to
leave home safely at short notice — a flood, fire, evacuation, or conflict. Keep it
light enough to carry and check it twice a year.

> **Tip:** Pack one bag per person who can carry one, and a smaller one for
> children. Keep copies of key documents in a sealed waterproof bag.

## Water and food

- [ ] Water bottle (and a small filter or purification tablets)
- [ ] High-energy food that needs no cooking (bars, nuts, dried fruit)
- [ ] Manual can opener if you pack tins

## Warmth and shelter

- [ ] Weatherproof jacket and a warm layer
- [ ] Emergency blanket or lightweight sleeping bag
- [ ] Sturdy shoes and spare socks
- [ ] Small tarp or poncho

## Light, power, and information

- [ ] Torch and spare batteries
- [ ] Wind-up or battery radio
- [ ] Power bank and charging cables
- [ ] Whistle to signal for help

## Health and hygiene

- [ ] First-aid kit (see the first-aid-kit checklist)
- [ ] Any regular medicines (a few days' supply)
- [ ] Soap, hand sanitiser, toothbrush
- [ ] Toilet paper and a few rubbish bags

## Documents and money

- [ ] ID, passports, and important documents (copies, sealed waterproof)
- [ ] Some cash in small notes and coins
- [ ] List of emergency contacts and meeting points
- [ ] Spare keys (home and vehicle)

## Tools and extras

- [ ] Multi-tool or pocket knife
- [ ] Lighter and waterproof matches
- [ ] Strong cord and duct tape
- [ ] Notebook and pencil
- [ ] Comfort items for children

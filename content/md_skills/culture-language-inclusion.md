# Culture and language inclusion

Apply this whenever an answer involves culture (music, dance, games,
storytelling) or language (phrases, literacy, communicating across barriers).

- [ ] **Everyone takes part.** Favour activities with no losers left out and no
      special equipment — circle dances, call-and-response songs, games where
      everyone keeps playing.
- [ ] **Meet people where they are.** Offer simpler steps, seated options, and
      gentle pacing for elders, children, and the less able.
- [ ] **Protect dignity.** Treat not reading, not knowing a song, or not sharing a
      language as ordinary — never as a failing to be mocked.
- [ ] **Respect other cultures and languages.** Encourage learning a few words of
      others' languages; share the effort of understanding both ways.
- [ ] **Culture carries values and memory.** Storytelling, songs, and games pass
      on skills, history, and how the community decides and shares — treat them as
      a way to keep knowledge alive, not just entertainment.
- [ ] **Keep it offline and local.** No apps or devices required; rely on people,
      voices, found materials, and a stick in the dirt.

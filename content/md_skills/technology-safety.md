# Local technology answer safety

Apply this whenever an answer involves radio, local networks, repurposed
computers, or other local tech infrastructure.

- [ ] **Community coordination, not tactical comms.** Frame radio and
      networking as everyday mutual aid and staying in touch — never as
      "OPSEC," secure comms, or combat/tactical radio procedure. horizon does
      not deal in defence or paramilitary framing.
- [ ] **Say what's regulated.** Two-way radio bands are licensed in most
      places (licence-free tiers like CB/FRS vs. licensed ham bands) — say so
      plainly rather than assuming any frequency is free to use.
- [ ] **No surveillance tooling.** Local networks and servers are for sharing
      information within a group by consent, never for monitoring, tracking,
      or spying on neighbours or group members.
- [ ] **Frame reuse, not upgrade.** Repurposing an old PC is about reducing
      e-waste and getting more life from what already exists — don't imply
      new hardware is needed when older hardware will do.
- [ ] **Be realistic about limits.** Local radio range, mesh network
      coverage, and old-hardware performance are all modest — set honest
      expectations rather than overselling range or capability.
- [ ] **Cite local guides** (the technology guides on radio, local networks,
      and repurposed servers) for the practical steps an answer depends on.

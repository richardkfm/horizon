# Emergency answer safety

Apply this whenever an answer involves emergencies (blackouts, extreme heat or
cold, air raids, armed conflict, pandemics, or other disasters).

- [ ] **Protect civilians; stay non-violent.** Frame everything as keeping
      ordinary people safe — avoiding harm, not causing it. Don't give offensive,
      weapon, or attack guidance.
- [ ] **Defer to official help.** Point people to local authorities, emergency
      services, and credible humanitarian guidance, which know the specific threat
      and the designated shelters.
- [ ] **Lead with the deadliest, least-obvious risks** up front — carbon monoxide
      from indoor generators/stoves, heatstroke, hypothermia, blast debris,
      unexploded ordnance.
- [ ] **Be calm and practical, not alarming.** Give clear, ordered steps people can
      act on, and reassure that preparation works (mirrors horizon's friendly,
      graceful-degradation tone).
- [ ] **Prioritise the basics:** water, warmth/cooling, clean air, distance from
      danger, information, and caring for the vulnerable.
- [ ] **Centre mutual aid.** Checking on neighbours and pooling resources is a core
      survival measure and reflects horizon's cooperative, anti-exploitation
      values.
- [ ] **Cite local guides** (water, health, shelter, low-tech solar) for the
      practical skills an emergency depends on.

# Calculation answer safety

Apply this whenever an answer involves practical calculations (energy sizing,
areas and volumes, material quantities, structural loads).

- [ ] **Show the method, not just a number.** Give the formula and the steps so
      the person can redo it with their own figures and learn it.
- [ ] **Keep units consistent and explicit.** Convert to one unit first, label
      every quantity with its unit, and remind that mixing units is the classic
      error.
- [ ] **Build in margins.** Recommend adding sensible headroom — energy losses,
      material waste, and especially structural safety factors. Round in the safe
      direction.
- [ ] **Be clear about limits, especially for structure.** Estimating methods suit
      small, simple builds. For anything large, multi-storey, public, or carrying
      many people or much weight, tell the person to use span tables, local
      building knowledge, or a qualified engineer — and to overbuild when unsure.
- [ ] **Sanity-check results.** Encourage comparing the answer to something known
      and asking whether it feels reasonable.
- [ ] **Cite local guides** (energy, shelter, water, food) so the numbers connect
      to the practical task.

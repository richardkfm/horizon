# Cooperative governance answer safety

Apply this whenever an answer involves group decisions, governance, resolving
conflict, sharing resources, or dealing with other groups.

- [ ] **Consensus over majority rule.** Treat genuine agreement — slow and hard
      as it is — as the basis of a just decision, not a headcount. A vote that
      steamrolls a minority is a last resort, not the default.
- [ ] **Protect the weak, silent, and minority voice.** Actively ask whether
      quieter people or those with less power have really been heard, not just
      whether the loudest are satisfied.
- [ ] **No permanent, unaccountable power.** Favour rotating roles, recall, and
      transparency over any position that can't be questioned or replaced.
- [ ] **Consent, not coercion.** Never recommend forcing, pressuring, or
      manipulating someone into going along with a group decision.
- [ ] **Fair, not exploitative, sharing.** When resources are scarce, prioritise
      need and transparency; never suggest using scarcity to gain leverage over
      people.
- [ ] **Be calm and practical.** Give concrete next steps for running a
      meeting, resolving a dispute, or agreeing a shared resource, not just
      abstract principle.
- [ ] **Cite local guides** (the cooperation guides on decisions, governance,
      conflict, sharing, and external relations) for the practical process an
      answer depends on.

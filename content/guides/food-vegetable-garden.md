# Grow a year-round vegetable garden

A mixed vegetable garden turns a small patch of ground into a steady supply of
fresh food. The goal is variety and *succession* — always having something coming
ready, rather than a single big glut.

> **Start small and succeed.** A well-tended small bed feeds you better than a
> large one you can't keep up with. Expand as your skill and compost grow.

## Plan the plot

- **Pick the spot:** most vegetables need 6+ hours of sun and shelter from strong
  wind. Near the kitchen and a water source is easier to tend.
- **Make beds you never step on** (about an arm's reach wide) so the soil stays
  loose and roots breathe.
- **Build the soil:** dig in compost, rotted manure, or leaf mould. Healthy soil
  is the whole game; feed it every season.

## What to grow

Mix fast and slow, and crops that store:

- **Quick and repeatable:** salad leaves, radish, spinach, beans.
- **Staple bulk:** potatoes, onions, squash, carrots, beets.
- **Cut-and-come-again:** chard, kale, herbs.
- **Climbers** (beans, peas, cucumbers) on supports to save space.

## Sow in succession

1. Sow small amounts **every few weeks** rather than all at once, so harvests are
   continuous.
2. As one crop finishes, **replant the space** straightaway.
3. **Rotate families** year to year (don't grow the same type in the same bed)
   to reduce pests and disease.
4. **Interplant** quick crops between slow ones to use every gap.

## Tend and protect

- **Water deeply and less often** at the roots, ideally in the cool of morning.
- **Mulch** bare soil to hold moisture and smother weeds.
- **Weed little and often** while weeds are small.
- **Watch for pests**; pick them off, encourage helpful insects, and net or fence
  against animals.

## Keep it going

Save seed from your best plants (see the seed-saving guide), keep a compost heap,
and note what did well each season so next year is easier.

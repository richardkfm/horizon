# Put out a fire safely

A small fire caught early can be put out with simple means; a growing fire will
outrun you. The most important survival skill here is knowing **when to fight and
when to flee** — your life is worth more than any building.

> **Do now:** If a fire is bigger than a wastebasket, spreading, or filling the room
> with smoke — get everyone out, close doors behind you, and call for help. Don't go
> back in.

> **Warning:** Smoke kills more people than flames. If you can't put a fire out in
> the first moments, leave. Never let a fire get between you and your way out.

## General rules

- **Match the method to the fuel** (see below) — water is right for some fires and
  dangerous for others.
- **Keep your exit behind you** so you can always back away.
- **Aim low, at the base** of the flames, not at the tops.
- **Smother and cool:** fire needs heat, fuel, and air. Remove any one and it dies —
  cool it with water, or cut off air by covering it.

## Cooking-oil / fat fire (the most common kitchen killer)

1. **Never use water** — it turns the oil to an explosive fireball.
2. **Turn off the heat** if you can reach it safely.
3. **Smother it:** slide a metal lid or a damp (wrung-out) cloth over the pan and
   leave it covered to cool. A fire blanket is ideal.
4. **Leave it covered** — don't move the hot pan or peek too soon.

## Electrical fire

1. **Cut the power** at the socket or fuse box if safe.
2. **Don't throw water** on live equipment — risk of shock.
3. Use a CO₂ or dry-powder extinguisher, or smother once the power is off.

## Burning solids (wood, paper, cloth)

- **Water works well** — soak it and the area around it, and stir/spread to reach
  embers. A dry-powder extinguisher also works.

## Chimney or wall fire

- **Get everyone out and call the fire service** — these spread inside the structure
  where you can't see or reach them. Don't try to fight a fire inside a wall or
  chimney yourself.

## Person on fire

**Stop, drop, and roll** — or smother the flames with a blanket or coat. Then cool
the burns with water and treat them (see the *burns* guide).

## Using an extinguisher — remember PASS

**P**ull the pin · **A**im at the base · **S**queeze the handle · **S**weep side to
side. Stand back a couple of metres, keep your exit behind you, and stop if the fire
doesn't quickly shrink.

## After a fire

Make sure it's fully out and won't reignite, ventilate smoke, and don't re-enter a
fire-damaged building until it's declared safe. See also the *wildfire* and *make
and keep a fire* guides.

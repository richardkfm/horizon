# Which crops should you plant?

Growing food is less about a "best" crop and more about matching the plant to
**your season, your climate, and what you need it for** — fast food now, calories
to live on, or a harvest that stores through winter.

> **Decide:** First name your goal. *Survive on it?* grow staples (calories that
> store). *Eat well soon?* grow fast greens. *Get through winter?* grow keepers.
> Most plots want some of each.

## Choose by your goal

| Your goal | Grow | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Calories to live on** | Potatoes, maize, beans, squash, sweet potato | Dense energy per square metre, many store well |
| **Food fast** | Lettuce, radish, spinach, rocket, bush beans | Ready in weeks, keep a plot productive |
| **Stores for winter** | Onions, garlic, dry beans, winter squash, carrots | Keep for months with little processing |
| **Protein** | Beans, peas, lentils, soy | Fix their own nitrogen, dry for storage |

> **Pick this if:** you are growing to *feed yourself* through a season — lead
> with **potatoes and beans**. They give the most calories and protein per
> square metre, tolerate poor soil, and store without a freezer.

## Choose by your season

Plant with the weather, not the calendar. The rule that travels everywhere:

| Plant when | Cool-season crops | Warm-season crops |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Soil is cold, frost still possible | Peas, broad beans, onions, spinach, brassicas | — |
| Soil has warmed, frost has passed | — | Tomatoes, maize, beans, squash, peppers |
| Late summer, for autumn/winter | Kale, garlic, winter salads, carrots | — |

> **Avoid if:** frost is still likely — do not sow warm-season crops (tomato,
> maize, squash, beans). A single frost kills them and wastes weeks. Wait for
> warm soil, or start them under cover.

## Choose by your climate

> **Spec:** A crop's **days to maturity** must fit inside your frost-free
> growing window. In a short season (≤120 frost-free days) choose quick or
> early varieties and start long-season crops indoors.

| Climate | Reliable choices | Avoid / protect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Short, cool summers | Potato, kale, peas, carrots, fast salads | Heat-lovers (melon, okra) without cover |
| Hot and dry | Sorghum, cowpea, squash, sweet potato | Thirsty salads in peak heat |
| Hot and wet / tropical | Cassava, sweet potato, taro, beans | Crops needing a cold spell |
| Temperate, four seasons | Almost anything in its season | Out-of-season planting |

## A rule of thumb for a first plot

1. **Half to staples** that store (potatoes, beans, squash) — your insurance.
2. **A quarter to fast food** (salads, radish) so you eat within weeks.
3. **A quarter to try** crops suited to your climate, and save seed from what does well.

> **Risk:** Planting only one crop is the real danger — a single pest, disease,
> or bad spell can take the lot. Mix crops and stagger sowings so a failure is a
> setback, not a famine.

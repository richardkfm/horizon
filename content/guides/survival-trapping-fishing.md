# Catch fish and small game for food

Plants and foraging cover much, but protein and fat are hard to get from greens
alone. Simple traps and fishing let a small amount of effort work for you while
you do other things — the key advantage of traps over hunting.

> **Know the law and the ethics.** In normal times, trapping, snaring, and
> fishing are often restricted by law and season — follow them. Always kill
> quickly and humanely, take only what you need, and never set traps where people
> or pets could be hurt. Treat this as survival knowledge, used responsibly.

## Let traps do the work

The advantage of traps is that many can work at once, day and night:

- **Snares:** a loop of wire or strong cord set on an animal trail or den
  entrance, anchored firmly. Look for runs, droppings, and tracks to place them.
- **Deadfalls:** a heavy weight propped to fall on triggered bait. Effective but
  dangerous to set — keep fingers and people clear.
- **Set many, check often:** more traps mean better odds; check them regularly so
  caught animals don't suffer or get taken by scavengers.

## Fishing without a rod

- **Handline:** a line, a hook (bent wire, bone, or a thorn gorge), and bait —
  worms, insects, scraps. Tie to a springy branch as a "set line" and leave it.
- **Fish traps:** a funnel of woven sticks or stones in a stream lets fish in but
  not out; or a basket trap baited and sunk. Passive and productive.
- **Stranding pools:** in shallows, build a low stone wall trapping fish as water
  drops.

- Look for fish near **cover, inflows, and shade**, most active at dawn and dusk.

## Other wild protein

- **Shellfish** in clean water, and freshwater crayfish under rocks, are easy to
  gather — but only from unpolluted water.
- **Insects** (grasshoppers, grubs) are abundant protein in many places; cook
  them and avoid brightly coloured, hairy, or foul-smelling ones.

## Handle catch safely

- **Bleed, gut, and cook promptly**; spoiled meat and fish cause serious illness.
- **Cook thoroughly** — wild meat and freshwater fish can carry parasites and
  disease.
- **Keep it clean:** wash hands and tools, and keep raw catch away from other
  food and water.

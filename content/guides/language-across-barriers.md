# Communicate across a language barrier

Sooner or later you will need to understand someone whose language you don't
share, with no phone or translator. It is very doable: humans communicated across
languages long before any technology, using patience, goodwill, and the body.

> **Assume good faith and stay calm.** Most misunderstanding is just that — not
> hostility. A warm, unhurried manner does half the work.

## Use what everyone shares

- **Gestures and mime:** point, act out the action (drinking, sleeping, walking),
  show quantities on fingers.
- **Facial expression and tone:** smiles, nods, and an open posture cross every
  language and lower fear.
- **Drawing:** sketch in the dirt or on any surface — a map, a sun for "day", a
  simple picture of what you mean. Pictures beat words for places and objects.
- **Objects:** hold up or point to the real thing whenever you can.

## Build understanding step by step

1. **Establish yes and no first** — agree on a gesture for each. Everything else
   builds on this.
2. **Exchange names**, pointing to yourselves.
3. **One idea at a time**, slowly. Don't pile on words; check you're understood
   before moving on.
4. **Confirm by repeating back** — act out or point to what you understood and
   watch for a yes or no.
5. **Be patient with silence;** give the other person time to work it out too.

## Grow a shared vocabulary

- Each time you agree on a word, **write both languages down** side by side, with
  a little drawing. This becomes a shared word list the whole group can use and
  grow (links to learning core phrases).
- Learn a few words of *their* language as they learn yours — sharing the effort
  builds trust and speeds things up.
- Keep a running picture-dictionary for the most-needed words: water, food,
  danger, help, trade, sick.

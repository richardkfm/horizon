# Stay safe during air raids and drone attacks

This is civilian-protection guidance: how ordinary people keep themselves and
their families safe during aerial bombardment, shelling, or drone attacks. The
core idea is simple and old — put distance and solid material between yourself and
the blast — and acting fast on a warning saves lives.

> **Follow official instructions first.** Local authorities and emergency services
> know the specific threat and the designated shelters. Their alerts and guidance
> override any general advice here.

## Know the warnings and plan ahead

- **Learn the alert signals** for your area (sirens, phone alerts, radio) and what
  each means.
- **Identify the safest place** in advance: a public shelter, a metro/underground
  station, a basement, or — failing that — the most protected spot in your home.
- **The "two walls" rule:** put as many solid walls as possible between you and
  the outside, away from windows. A central room, hallway, or stairwell on a low
  floor is usually safest.
- **Prepare a small kit** by the shelter spot: water, torch, radio, first-aid,
  warm layers, important documents, and any medicines.

## When the alert sounds

1. **Go to your safe place immediately** — calmly but without delay. Minutes
   matter; don't stop to gather belongings.
2. **Get low and away from windows and exterior walls.** Glass and flying debris
   cause most injuries.
3. **Take cover under something sturdy** if you can, and protect your head and
   neck.
4. **Stay inside and under cover** until an official all-clear — secondary strikes
   can follow the first.

## During drone or loitering-munition threats

- **Get indoors and under solid cover;** do not stand in the open to look or film.
- **Keep away from windows;** lower floors and interior rooms give more protection.
- **Reduce light at night** if authorities advise, and follow local guidance on
  movement.

## After it passes

- **Wait for the all-clear**, then check yourself and others for injuries and give
  first aid.
- **Watch for hazards:** unexploded ordnance, gas leaks, fire, damaged
  structures, and downed power lines. **Do not touch suspicious objects** — report
  them to authorities.
- **Help neighbours**, especially anyone trapped, injured, or alone, and follow
  official channels for what to do next.

# Handle common first-aid situations

Most everyday emergencies are handled with a calm head, a few supplies, and a
simple order of priorities. This guide is a starting point, not a substitute for
hands-on training — take a first-aid course when you can.

> **Risk:** First aid buys time; it does not replace professional care. For
> serious bleeding, breathing trouble, chest pain, stroke signs, or a
> non-responsive person, get emergency help as your first action where it is
> available. When unsure, treat it as serious.

## A basic kit

- Sterile gauze, non-stick dressings, and a range of plasters
- Adhesive tape, crepe/elastic bandage, and a triangular bandage
- Disposable gloves and antiseptic wipes
- Tweezers, scissors, and safety pins
- A digital thermometer and a foil/space blanket
- Oral rehydration salts and any personal medicines (with a written list)

## A calm order of priorities

1. **Make the scene safe** for you first — you are no help as a second casualty.
2. **Check responsiveness and breathing.** Not breathing normally → start CPR and
   call for help.
3. **Stop serious bleeding:** firm, direct pressure with a clean pad; keep it
   pressed and add layers rather than peeking.
4. **Treat for shock:** lay them down, keep them warm and reassured.
5. **Then deal with lesser injuries** once life-threats are handled.

## Common situations

- **Minor cuts/grazes:** clean with safe water, stop bleeding, cover.
- **Burns:** cool under safe running water for ~20 minutes, then cover loosely
  with non-stick dressing. Don't apply creams, ice, or pop blisters.
- **Sprains:** rest, a cool pack, gentle compression, and elevation.
- **Dehydration / diarrhoea:** sip oral rehydration salts; seek help if it
  persists, especially in the very young or old.
- **Fever:** rest and fluids; watch for stiff neck, rash, or breathing trouble,
  which need urgent help.

## Build the habit

- Keep the kit stocked, dry, and where everyone can find it.
- Note allergies and medicines for each household member.
- Learn CPR and the recovery position in person before you need them.

# Stay safe in a nuclear or radiological emergency

A release from a nuclear plant or a radiological accident sounds terrifying, but
the protective actions are clear and effective, and ordinary buildings give real
protection. The three words to remember are **Get inside, stay inside, stay
tuned.**

> **Follow the authorities above all.** Officials know the specific release, the
> wind, and the danger zone, and will say whether to **shelter** or **evacuate**,
> and whether to take **iodine**. This guide prepares you to act on their
> instructions — it does not replace them.

## The three principles of protection

Radiation exposure drops with:

- **Time** — less time near the source means less dose.
- **Distance** — the farther away, the better.
- **Shielding** — put mass (walls, earth, concrete) between you and it.

## Get inside, stay inside

1. **Get indoors fast** — a solid building, ideally brick or concrete; the more
   walls and floors around you, the better.
2. **Go to the middle** — a basement or a central room on a middle floor, away
   from outside walls and the roof.
3. **Seal the room:** close and lock windows and doors, and **turn off and block
   ventilation** (fans, air conditioning, vents, chimneys) to keep contaminated
   air out.
4. **Stay tuned** to radio or official channels and wait for instructions; stay
   inside until told it's safe — often hours to a couple of days.

## If you were outside during the release

1. **Cover your nose and mouth** on the way in.
2. **Remove your outer clothing once inside** — this removes the large majority of
   any contamination. Bag it and set it well aside.
3. **Wash gently** — shower or wipe skin and hair with water and mild soap; **don't
   scrub or use conditioner** (it binds contamination to hair). Blow your nose,
   wipe eyelids and ears.

## Food, water, and iodine

- **Eat and drink only sealed or already-indoor food and water.** Covered wells,
  sealed containers, and packaged food are safest; **avoid fresh local produce,
  milk, and open water** until authorities clear them.
- **Potassium iodide (KI)** protects only the thyroid from radioactive iodine, and
  only helps around the time of exposure. **Take it only when officials say to,**
  at the correct dose for age — it is not a shield against radiation generally and
  has risks if misused.

## Urban vs out of town

- **In town:** dense, solid buildings shelter well; **don't use lifts** if power
  is unstable, and follow crowd-control and evacuation routing carefully.
- **Out of town / downwind of a plant:** you may be in the path of the plume.
  **Bring livestock under cover, cover feed and water, and protect wells**;
  **farm produce and milk** are the main contamination route, so follow guidance
  on what is safe to consume and be ready to evacuate if directed.

## Evacuate only when told

Sheltering is often safest at first. **Evacuate only on official instruction or if
staying is clearly more dangerous** — then go quickly, by the route given, keeping
the household together (see *Keep your household safe during armed conflict* for
go-bag and evacuation basics).

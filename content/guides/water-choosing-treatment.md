# Which water treatment should you use?

There is no single "best" way to make water safe — the right choice depends on
**what is wrong with your water**, how much you need, and what you have to hand.
This guide helps you *choose*; the step-by-step builds live in the linked guides.

> **Decide:** Treatment removes different threats. Sediment and cloudiness need
> *filtering*; living things (bacteria, viruses, parasites) need *disinfection*;
> chemicals and salt need *distillation*. Most real situations need two steps —
> clear the water first, then disinfect it.

## What is in your water?

| Threat | You might see / suspect | What actually removes it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sediment, mud, cloudiness | Murky, coloured, gritty water | Settling, cloth/sand pre-filter |
| Bacteria & parasites | Animal/human activity upstream | Slow sand filter, boiling, chlorine, solar (SODIS) |
| Viruses | Sewage, dense settlement nearby | Boiling, chlorine (not most simple filters) |
| Chemicals, salt, heavy metals | Industry, mining, seawater, runoff | **Distillation only** — filtering won't help |

> **Risk:** No simple field method removes everything. If you suspect chemical
> or industrial contamination, do not drink it — distil it or find another
> source. When in doubt, treat for living things *and* keep testing.

## Compare the common methods

| Method | Good against | Throughput | Needs | Best when |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Boiling** | Bacteria, viruses, parasites | Small batches | Fuel, a pot | You have fuel and need water now |
| **Chlorine / bleach drops** | Bacteria, viruses | Any volume | Unscented bleach, a measure | Treating stored or transported water |
| **SODIS (sun in clear bottles)** | Bacteria, viruses, parasites | ~1–2 L/bottle/day | Clear PET bottles, strong sun | Hot, sunny climates, no fuel |
| **Slow sand filter** | Bacteria, parasites, cloudiness | Steady for a group | Sand, a barrel, weeks to ripen | A standing supply for ~20 people |
| **Distillation** | Everything, incl. salt & chemicals | Very small | Heat, condensing surface | Seawater or chemical contamination |

> **Pick this if:** you must serve a small group day after day from a stream or
> pond — build a **slow sand filter** to clear and biologically treat the water,
> then add a final disinfection step (boil or chlorinate) before drinking.

> **Avoid if:** the water could carry chemicals or salt — sand filters,
> ceramic filters and cloth do nothing for those. Reach for distillation
> instead, or change source.

## A quick rule of thumb

1. **Cloudy?** Let it settle, then filter through cloth or sand until it runs clear.
2. **From nature?** Assume it carries living things — disinfect by boiling,
   chlorine, or strong sun.
3. **Industry, mining, or sea nearby?** Assume chemicals or salt — distil, or
   find another source.
4. **Unsure?** Combine *clear, then disinfect*, and test the result.

> **Spec:** Chlorine dose for clear water is roughly **2 drops of unscented 5%
> household bleach per litre** (4 if cloudy); mix, then wait **30 minutes**.
> Boiling: bring to a **rolling boil for 1 minute** (3 minutes above ~2000 m).

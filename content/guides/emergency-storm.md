# Stay safe in severe storms

Severe storms — hurricanes and cyclones, tornadoes, violent thunderstorms — bring
several dangers at once: high wind, flying debris, lightning, and often flooding.
Warnings usually give you some time, so use it: secure your surroundings and know
where you'll shelter.

> **Know the difference: watch vs warning.** A *watch* means conditions are
> possible — prepare. A *warning* means it's happening or imminent — **act now**
> and take shelter. Don't wait to see the storm.

## Prepare when a storm is coming

- **Secure the outside:** bring in or tie down anything the wind can throw — furniture,
  tools, bins; trim weak branches ahead of the season.
- **Ready supplies:** water, food, medicines, torch, battery radio, charged power
  banks, and a go-bag in case you must leave.
- **Protect openings:** close and, for severe wind, board or shutter windows.
- **Know your shelter** in the building and your evacuation route if one is
  ordered.

## High winds, hurricanes, and cyclones

1. **If evacuation is ordered** (especially for coastal **storm surge** — the
   biggest killer in hurricanes), **leave early** by the route given.
2. **If sheltering:** stay in a **small, windowless interior room on the lowest
   safe floor**, away from glass.
3. **Beware the calm "eye"** of a hurricane — the wind returns suddenly from the
   other direction. Stay sheltered until officials say it's over.

## Tornadoes

- **Go low and central:** a basement or storm cellar is best; otherwise a small
  interior room (bathroom, closet) on the lowest floor, away from windows. Cover
  your head and neck.
- **Mobile homes and vehicles are not safe** — get to a sturdy building or, as a
  last resort, lie low in a ditch away from cars and trees.

## Lightning

- **"When thunder roars, go indoors."** Lightning can strike from miles away.
  Shelter in a solid building or a hard-topped vehicle.
- **Outdoors with no shelter:** avoid high ground, lone trees, open fields, and
  water; don't be the tallest thing around. Wait **30 minutes after the last
  thunder** before going back out.

## Urban vs out of town

- **In town:** the danger is **flying glass and debris, downed power lines, and
  blocked drains/flash flooding** — stay away from windows and never touch downed
  lines. Expect power cuts (see *Prepare for and live through a long blackout*).
- **Out of town:** you're more **exposed and more likely to be cut off** by fallen
  trees and flooded roads — shelter away from large trees, secure or move
  livestock, and keep several days of supplies.

## After the storm

Watch for **downed power lines** (treat all as live), gas leaks, weakened trees
and structures, and **floodwater** (see *Stay safe in a flood*). Help neighbours,
especially anyone alone or vulnerable, and wait for the all-clear before
travelling.

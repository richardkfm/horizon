# Survive extreme heat without power

Extreme heat kills more people than most disasters, quietly and indoors. Without
air conditioning, staying safe is about lowering your body's heat load: shade,
air, water, and rest. It is very manageable if you act early.

> **Know the danger signs.** Heat exhaustion — heavy sweating, dizziness, nausea,
> headache, cramps — can tip into **heatstroke**: hot dry skin, confusion,
> collapse. Heatstroke is a life-threatening emergency: cool the person fast and
> get medical help.

## Keep your home cooler

- **Block the sun by day:** close curtains, blinds, or shutters on sunlit
  windows; cover them outside with cloth or boards if you can.
- **Open up at night:** when the outside air is cooler than inside, open windows
  and create a through-draught to flush heat out, then close up before the day
  heats again.
- **Make a breeze:** a battery or solar fan helps; a damp cloth hung in a draught
  cools the air passing through it.
- **Stay low and shaded:** heat rises; the coolest part of a building is usually
  downstairs or a basement.

## Keep your body cool

- **Drink water steadily** through the day, before you feel thirsty. Avoid alcohol
  and a lot of caffeine.
- **Wet your skin:** a damp cloth on the neck, wrists, and face; a cool shower or
  foot soak. Wear loose, light, light-coloured clothing.
- **Slow down:** rest in the hottest hours (often midday to late afternoon); do
  any hard work early morning or evening.
- **Eat light** and replace salt lost in sweat with food if you're sweating a lot.

## Protect the vulnerable

Older people, babies and young children, pregnant people, the unwell, and outdoor
workers are most at risk:

- **Check on them often** — at least twice a day in a heatwave.
- Make sure they are **drinking and staying cool**; move them to the coolest
  space.
- **Never leave anyone — or a pet — in a parked vehicle**, even briefly.

## If someone overheats

1. Move them to the **coolest place** you can.
2. **Cool them actively:** wet skin, fan them, cold packs to neck, armpits, groin.
3. **Give sips of water** if they're conscious and able to swallow.
4. If they're confused, vomiting, or collapse, treat it as **heatstroke** — cool
   aggressively and get emergency help immediately.

# Plant fruit trees and berry bushes

Fruit trees and berry bushes are among the best returns on effort in any garden:
plant once, then harvest for years or decades with modest care. They need
patience — most take a few seasons to bear well — so the best time to plant was
years ago, and the next best time is now.

> **Plant for your place.** Choose types and varieties suited to your climate,
> soil, and space. Local knowledge and local plants beat exotic ones that
> struggle.

## Choose what to plant

- **Trees:** apple, pear, plum, cherry, fig, citrus, and others to suit your
  climate. Check the expected **size** and pick dwarf/semi-dwarf kinds for small
  spaces.
- **Bushes and canes:** currants, gooseberries, raspberries, blackberries,
  blueberries — quick to fruit and easy to fit in.
- **Vines:** grapes and kiwi on a fence or frame.
- **Pollination:** some fruit needs a second compatible variety nearby to set
  fruit — check before you plant just one.

## Plant well

1. **Plant in the dormant season** (autumn–early spring in most climates).
2. Dig a hole **wider than deep**; loosen the sides so roots can spread.
3. Set the plant at the **same depth it grew before** (don't bury the graft union
   on grafted trees).
4. Backfill, firm gently, **water in well**, and mulch — keeping mulch off the
   trunk.
5. **Stake** young trees against wind, and protect bark from animals.

## Care through the years

- **Water** regularly for the first two years while roots establish.
- **Prune** in the right season to shape the plant, let in light and air, and
  remove dead or crossing wood.
- **Feed** with compost or manure each year and keep the base weed-free.
- **Thin heavy fruit** so branches don't break and fruit grows larger.

## Harvest and beyond

Pick when ripe, and preserve gluts by drying, fermenting, or storing in a cool
place. Take cuttings or grow from suckers to spread your favourites for free.

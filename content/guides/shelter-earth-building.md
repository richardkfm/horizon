# Build with earth (cob, adobe, earthbag)

Earth is the oldest and most widespread building material on Earth: cheap or
free, local, fireproof, and excellent at evening out temperature. With the right
soil and good detailing, earth walls last generations.

> **Keep earth dry.** Earth's one weakness is standing water. The old builders'
> rule is "a good hat and good boots": a wide roof overhang above and a solid,
> raised stone or rubble foundation below.

## Test your soil

Earth building needs the right mix of clay (the glue) and sand/aggregate (the
strength):

- **Jar test:** shake soil with water in a jar and let it settle; the layers show
  roughly how much sand, silt, and clay you have.
- **Ball test:** a damp handful should hold together but not be sticky like pure
  clay. Add sand if too sticky, clay if too crumbly.
- Avoid topsoil with lots of organic matter — dig down to subsoil.

## Choose a method

- **Cob:** clay-rich soil mixed with sand and straw, built up by hand in
  sculptural, monolithic walls. No forms needed; very forgiving.
- **Adobe:** the same mix formed into bricks, sun-dried, then laid with earth
  mortar. Good where you can make many bricks ahead of time.
- **Earthbag:** woven bags filled with damp soil (or gravel for the base course),
  stacked like bricks and tamped, with barbed wire between courses. Fast, strong,
  and good for curved walls.
- **Rammed earth:** damp soil compacted in hard layers inside temporary
  formwork. Very durable but needs more equipment.

## Build the wall

1. **Foundation first:** a raised footing of stone, rubble, or gravel-filled bags
   keeps ground moisture out of the earth above.
2. **Build up in layers**, letting cob/rammed work firm before adding much more
   height so walls don't slump.
3. **Set door and window frames** as you build, tying them into the wall.
4. **Keep walls plumb and thick** — thick walls are stronger and better
   insulated.

## Protect and finish

- **Roof with a generous overhang** to keep rain off the walls.
- **Plaster** with an earth or lime render to shed weather; earth plaster is
  repairable, lime is tougher against rain.
- **Maintain yearly:** patch cracks and re-coat plaster — easy with earth, and
  part of how these buildings last so long.

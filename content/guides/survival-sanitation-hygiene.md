# Manage waste and hygiene without plumbing

More people are made ill by poor sanitation after a disaster than by the
disaster itself — contaminated water is usually the cause, and human waste is
usually the source. Managing it properly, from the first day, is one of the
highest-value things you can do for a group's health.

> **Decide:** Site any latrine or waste pit at least 60 m (about 200 steps)
> from any water source, and always downhill and downwind of both the water
> and where people sleep and cook. Uphill or upstream of water is never
> acceptable, even for one use.

## Dig a latrine

**For a short stay or one person:** a cathole works —

1. Dig a hole 15–20 cm deep, away from camp and water as above.
2. Use it, then fill it back in completely with the dug soil.
3. Move to a fresh spot each time rather than reusing one hole.

**For a group or a longer stay:** dig a trench latrine —

1. Dig a trench roughly 30 cm wide, 30–45 cm deep, and as long as the group's
   stay will need.
2. Keep a scoop of soil or ash next to it; add a scoop after each use to cut
   smell and keep flies away.
3. Add a simple screen for privacy and a lid or cover (board, tarp) to keep
   rain and flies out between uses.
4. When it's nearly full, backfill it completely with soil, mound and mark
   it, and dig a new trench nearby.

> **Risk:** Never site a latrine where rain can wash it into a stream, well,
> or storage tank. Waste that reaches drinking water is exactly how outbreaks
> of diarrhoeal disease start after a flood or long outage.

## Handle greywater (washing and dish water)

- **Never pour greywater straight into or near a clean water source.**
- **Strain out food scraps first** (they attract animals and insects), then
  tip the water into a small dug soakage pit well away from any water source,
  or onto ground that won't run off into one.
- **Keep food-prep water separate from wash water** where you can, and wash
  hands and dishes downhill of where you collect drinking water.

## Wash hands without running water

A simple "tippy-tap" keeps handwashing going with almost no water:

1. Hang a container with a small hole or spigot near head height, above a
   drainage spot.
2. Tip or tap it to release a trickle over your hands — a little soap or ash
   and a rub is enough; you don't need a running stream.
3. Refill from any water, treated or not — handwashing water doesn't need to
   be drinking-quality, only kept away from food and mouths.

> **Tip:** Wash hands after using the latrine and before handling food or
> water, every time. This one habit prevents more illness than almost
> anything else on this list.

## If someone in camp is sick

- **Keep their waste separate** — their own latrine or cathole if possible,
  well away from the shared one.
- **Wash your hands** after any contact with them or their waste, and clean
  any surfaces they've used.
- See the disease-outbreak guide for wider precautions if illness is
  spreading through the group.

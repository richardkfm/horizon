# Mend clothes and make basic textiles

When new clothing is hard to get, mending is wealth: a few simple stitches keep
garments, blankets, and bags in service for years. These skills need only a
needle, thread, and patience.

> **Mend early.** A small tear caught now takes one minute; left alone it becomes
> a big hole and a ruined garment. Check clothes regularly.

## Your basic kit

- A **needle** (steel, or improvised from bone, thorn, or wire with an eye).
- **Thread** — salvaged thread, unravelled yarn, or thin plant cordage.
- **Scissors or a sharp blade**, and pins or thorns to hold cloth in place.

## Core repairs

- **Running stitch:** small in-and-out stitches in a line — the basic seam.
- **Backstitch:** stronger; each stitch doubles back over the last. Use it for
  seams under strain.
- **Whipstitch (overcast):** loops over an edge to join two pieces or stop fraying.
- **Sew on a button:** stitch through the holes several times, wrap the thread
  under the button a few times to make a "shank", then knot off.
- **Patch a hole:** cut a patch larger than the hole from spare cloth, fold under
  its edges, pin over the hole, and stitch all around — on the inside for
  strength, the outside for speed.
- **Darn a hole** (knits/socks): weave new thread across the hole in one
  direction, then weave over and under at right angles to fill it.

## Make simple textiles

- **Sew flat items** — bags, pouches, simple garments — by stitching rectangles of
  cloth together inside-out, then turning right-side out.
- **Reinforce stress points** (corners, straps, knees, elbows) with extra
  stitching or a patch before they wear through.

## Care that prevents repairs

- Wash gently and dry out of harsh sun where you can.
- Keep cloth dry and away from rodents and damp; store folded and aired.
- Pass on the skill — teach children to thread a needle and fix a seam.

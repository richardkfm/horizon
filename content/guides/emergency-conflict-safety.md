# Keep your household safe during armed conflict

When conflict reaches where you live — invasion, civil unrest, or war — the
priority for ordinary people is staying out of harm's way and keeping the family
together and supplied. This is non-combatant survival: avoiding danger, not
seeking it.

> **This is protective, not political.** The aim is to keep civilians alive and
> together. Stay away from fighting, avoid taking sides in ways that put your
> household at risk, and follow credible official and humanitarian guidance.

## Prepare before things worsen

- **Pack a go-bag** for each person you can carry: water and treatment, some food,
  warm and weatherproof clothes, sturdy shoes, first-aid kit and medicines, torch,
  power bank, whistle, cash, and copies of key documents.
- **Documents:** keep ID, property and medical papers together and protected
  (waterproof bag), plus photos/scans stored separately and a written list of key
  contacts and account numbers.
- **Agree a family plan:** where to meet if separated, who to contact, and at
  least two routes out of your area. Make sure children and elders know it.
- **Stock the home** with water, food, medicines, and fuel for sheltering in
  place, in case leaving isn't safe.

## Communication

- **Keep phones charged** and have a battery/wind-up radio for news when networks
  fail.
- **Agree an out-of-area contact** everyone can check in with, since local lines
  may be overwhelmed.
- **Be careful what you share** about your location and movements; protect your
  family's privacy and safety.

## Shelter in place or evacuate

- **Stay put if moving is more dangerous** than staying: shelter in a protected
  interior room away from windows (see the air-raid guide), ration supplies, and
  wait for reliable instructions.
- **Evacuate if told to, or if staying is clearly unsafe** — and do it early,
  before routes are crowded or cut. Travel light, in daylight where possible, on
  routes authorities deem safe, and keep the family together.
- **At checkpoints and around armed groups:** stay calm, keep hands visible, follow
  instructions, carry your ID, and don't make sudden movements.

## Stay safe and human

- **Avoid crowds, fighting, and demonstrations** that can turn violent.
- **Watch for hazards:** unexploded ordnance, mines, damaged buildings, and looting
  — don't touch suspicious objects, and report them if you safely can.
- **Look after each other:** pool resources with trusted neighbours, share
  information, and care for the wounded, children, and the elderly. Mutual aid is
  what carries communities through conflict.
- **Seek out humanitarian help** — aid agencies, medics, and registration points —
  for shelter, supplies, and reuniting with separated family.

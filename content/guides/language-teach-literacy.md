# Teach basic reading and writing

Reading and writing let people keep records, share knowledge across distance and
time, and take part fully in decisions. You can teach the basics with nothing but
a stick and smooth ground, patience, and a little structure.

> **Dignity always.** Learners of any age may feel ashamed not to read. Be
> patient and private, celebrate small wins, and never mock a mistake.

## Start with sounds, then letters

Most writing systems map sounds to symbols. Build from there:

1. **Listen first:** play games hearing the sounds in words — what does "water"
   start with? What's the last sound in "fire"?
2. **Teach letter shapes with their sounds**, a few at a time. Trace them big in
   sand, ash, or dust before using precious paper.
3. **Blend sounds into words:** sound out short, useful words slowly, then faster
   — c-a-t, w-a-t-e-r.
4. **Read real, useful words first:** names, numbers, labels for stores and
   medicines, the core survival phrases.

## Writing follows reading

- Have learners copy letters they can already read, large and slow.
- Move from letters to their own name, then to short words and lists.
- Let them write things that matter to them — a name, a tally, a message.

## Make materials from nothing

- **Write surfaces:** smooth earth, sand trays, a charcoal stick on a board or
  flat stone, a wet finger on stone.
- **Flashcards:** scraps of bark, cloth, or salvaged card with one word each.
- **A word wall:** post useful words where people pass them daily.

## Keep it going

- **Short, frequent sessions** (15-20 minutes) beat rare long ones.
- **Pair learners** so they practise together between sessions.
- **Train new teachers** as learners advance — each one teach one — so literacy
  spreads through the community on its own.

# Help someone choking or not breathing

When someone can't breathe, every second counts. The two situations here are
**choking** (something blocking the airway) and **cardiac arrest** (no breathing,
no response). Both need fast, simple action.

> **Do now:** If someone collapses and isn't breathing normally, send for help and
> start chest compressions: push hard and fast in the centre of the chest.

> **Warning:** First aid only. Get professional help immediately — phone emergency
> services if you possibly can, or send someone to fetch help while you act. If you
> can take a hands-on first-aid course, do; practice makes this far easier.

## Choking — when they can still cough

If the person can cough, speak, or breathe, **encourage them to keep coughing** —
coughing is the most effective way to clear a blockage. Stay with them.

## Choking — when they can't breathe, cough, or speak

1. **Give up to 5 back blows.** Lean them forward and strike firmly between the
   shoulder blades with the heel of your hand.
2. **Give up to 5 abdominal thrusts.** Stand behind, make a fist just above the
   navel, grasp it with your other hand, and pull sharply inwards and upwards.
3. **Alternate** 5 back blows and 5 thrusts until the object comes out or the
   person becomes unresponsive.

For a baby under one year, use 5 back blows and 5 **chest** thrusts (two fingers on
the breastbone) instead of abdominal thrusts.

## If they become unresponsive — start CPR

1. **Check response and breathing.** Tap them, shout. If there's no normal
   breathing, begin CPR.
2. **Send for help** — get someone to call emergency services and find a
   defibrillator (AED) if one exists nearby.
3. **Chest compressions:** heel of your hand in the centre of the chest, other hand
   on top. Push down hard about 5–6 cm, then let the chest rise fully. Aim for
   about 100–120 pushes a minute.
4. **Rescue breaths (if trained and willing):** after 30 compressions, give 2
   breaths, tilting the head back and lifting the chin. If you can't or won't,
   **keep doing continuous compressions** — that alone saves lives.
5. **Keep going** without stopping until help arrives, an AED is ready, or the
   person starts breathing normally.

## After

If they recover and are breathing, place them on their side (recovery position),
keep them warm, and stay with them until help arrives.

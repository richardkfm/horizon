# Find direction without a compass

Knowing which way is which lets you reach help, return to camp, and avoid walking
in circles. None of this needs a compass — just the sky and some care.

> **Before you move:** Tell someone your plan, or leave a marker. If you are
> lost and have shelter, water, and people may search for you, often the safest
> choice is to **stay put** and signal.

## By the sun (daytime)

- The sun rises roughly in the **east** and sets roughly in the **west**.
- **Shadow-stick method:** push a stick upright into level ground and mark the
  tip of its shadow. Wait 15-20 minutes and mark the new tip. The first mark is
  **west**, the second **east**; the line between them runs roughly east-west,
  and north is at right angles to it.
- An analogue watch can help, but the shadow-stick works anywhere.

```ascii
                         N
                         |
                         |
            W o----------+----------o E
          (1st mark)     |      (2nd mark, ~15-20 min later)
                         |
                         S

                    * stick (upright,
                      planted at the +)
```

*Fig. 1: shadow-stick method, viewed from above — the line from the first shadow-tip mark (west) to the second (east) gives an east-west line; north is at right angles to it*

## By the stars (clear night)

- **Northern Hemisphere:** find the Big Dipper; the two stars at the end of its
  "cup" point to **Polaris**, the North Star, which marks **north**.
- **Southern Hemisphere:** find the Southern Cross; extend its long axis about
  4.5 times its length to an empty point — straight down from there is **south**.

## Keep a straight line

- Pick two landmarks ahead in line (a tree, then a hill behind it). Walk to the
  first, line up the next pair, and repeat. This stops you drifting.
- In featureless terrain, send someone ahead to line up, or check your back trail
  often.

## Read the land

- Water flows downhill; following a stream downstream often leads to people.
- Note unmistakable features (a ridge, a river) as a "handrail" you can always
  return to.

## Where to go next

- If you decide to stay put rather than move, make yourself easy to find:
  see signal for rescue if lost or stranded.

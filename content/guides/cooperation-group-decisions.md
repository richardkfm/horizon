# Make fair group decisions

Rebuilding is a group effort. How a group decides is as important as what it
decides: fair, transparent processes build the trust that keeps people
cooperating voluntarily.

> **Principle:** Prefer consent and broad agreement over domination by the loud
> or the powerful. No one should be coerced; everyone affected should have a real
> voice.

## A simple decision process

1. **Frame the decision** clearly: what are we deciding, and why now?
2. **Gather perspectives:** make space for quieter voices before louder ones.
3. **Surface proposals** and the needs behind them.
4. **Test for consent:** ask not "who's in favour?" but "can you live with this,
   and if not, what's your concern?"
5. **Integrate objections** into an improved proposal where possible.
6. **Decide and record** what was agreed, who does what, and when to review it.

## Resolving conflict

- Separate people from the problem; focus on needs, not positions.
- Let each side restate the other's view until it's recognised as fair.
- Look for options that meet the core needs of everyone, not a "winner".
- Agree on a concrete next step and a time to check back in.

## Sharing resources fairly

- Make who-gets-what transparent and revisable.
- Weight access toward need and contribution without creating permanent hierarchy.
- Keep a path for anyone to raise that the arrangement has stopped being fair.

# Build a simple timber-frame cabin

A small timber cabin is a sound, dry, longer-term home you can build with hand
tools and local wood. The principle is simple: a strong frame of posts and beams,
clad and roofed to keep weather out, raised off the ground to stay dry.

> **Dry wood lasts; wet wood rots.** Keep the structure off the ground, give the
> roof a good overhang, and let air move around the timber. Most cabin failures
> are water failures.

## Plan and prepare

- **Keep it small and square** — a simple rectangle is far easier to frame and
  roof than anything clever.
- **Source timber:** straight, seasoned (dried) wood is strongest; green wood will
  shrink and twist as it dries, so allow for it.
- **Pick a dry, level, well-drained spot**, ideally with morning sun.

## Build from the ground up

1. **Foundation:** set posts on flat stones, concrete pads, or short piers so no
   wood touches the soil. Level them carefully — everything builds on this.
2. **Floor frame:** lay beams across the foundations, add joists, and deck over
   with boards. A level, solid floor makes the rest straightforward.
3. **Walls:** raise corner posts, connect them with top and bottom plates, and
   add upright studs between. Brace corners diagonally so the frame can't rack
   (lean) sideways.
4. **Roof:** set a ridge beam and rafters, or use simple triangular trusses.
   Pitch it enough to shed rain and snow, with an **overhang** past the walls.

## Close it in

- **Roof covering first** so you can work dry: boards plus a waterproof layer
  (shingles, metal, thatch, or salvaged sheeting), overlapped top-down.
- **Clad the walls** with overlapping boards (shed water downward), or infill the
  frame with earth/straw for insulation.
- **Insulate** walls, floor, and roof against heat loss (see the insulation
  guide), and seal gaps against draughts.
- **Fit a door and windows**, framed square and weather-sealed.

## Make it livable and last

- Add a **safe stove or hearth** with a proper, fireproof flue — never improvise
  this; bad flues cause fires and poisoning.
- **Ventilate** to prevent damp and condensation.
- **Inspect yearly** for leaks, rot, and pests, and treat or replace wood early.

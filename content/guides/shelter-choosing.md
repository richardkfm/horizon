# Which shelter should you build?

A shelter is a trade-off between **how fast you can build it** and **how long it
will serve you**. An emergency lean-to you can throw up in an hour is the wrong
choice for a winter you must overwinter in — and a timber cabin is the wrong
choice when night is falling and it is already cold.

> **Decide:** Answer three questions first — *How long do I need it?* *What is
> the climate?* *What can I build with what I have and know?* The longest-lasting
> option you can actually finish in time is usually the right one.

## Choose by how long you need it

| Need it for | Build | Time to build |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tonight / a few days | Lean-to, debris hut, tarp shelter | Under a day |
| Weeks to a season | Insulated frame, earth-bermed shelter | Days |
| Months to years | Earth (cob/adobe), timber-frame cabin | Weeks+ |

> **Pick this if:** night is coming and you are exposed — build the **quickest
> warm, dry, small** shelter you can (debris hut or lean-to with a thick bed and
> roof). A small space you can heat with your body beats a grand plan you can't
> finish before dark.

> **Avoid if:** you will be here for months — a flimsy emergency shelter wastes
> heat and rots. Invest the days in insulation and mass instead.

## Choose by climate

| Climate | Priority | Good choices |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cold | Trap heat, block wind, insulate the ground | Small earth-bermed or heavily-insulated frame; debris hut short-term |
| Hot & dry | Shade, thermal mass, ventilation | Earth/cob walls (cool by day, warm by night) |
| Hot & wet | Airflow, rain shedding, off the ground | Raised timber frame with a big roof overhang |
| Wet & windy | Stay dry, anchor against wind | Steep-roofed frame, well-sealed, sheltered aspect |

> **Spec:** In the cold, the ground steals more heat than the air. Put **at least
> 15–20 cm of dry insulation (leaves, grass, boughs) under you** before you worry
> about walls — a raised or insulated bed is the single biggest warmth win.

## Choose by materials and skill

| You have | Lowest-effort durable option |
| --- | --- |
| Earth/clay subsoil + time | Cob or adobe — strong, fireproof, free |
| Timber + tools + saw skills | Timber-frame cabin |
| Mostly forest debris | Debris hut / lean-to (short-term) |
| Salvage (tarps, sheet, pallets) | Insulated frame clad in salvage |

> **Risk:** Fire and ventilation kill more than cold. If you heat or cook inside
> any shelter, you **must** ventilate — carbon monoxide is silent. Keep flame
> away from dry debris roofing.

## A rule of thumb

1. **Get safe tonight** with the fastest warm, dry, small shelter.
2. **Upgrade in place** as days allow — add insulation, then mass, then space.
3. **Build for the climate**, not the picture in your head.

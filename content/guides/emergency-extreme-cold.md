# Survive extreme cold without heating

When heating fails in deep cold, the danger builds slowly and can be deadly. The
strategy is to trap your own body heat and shrink the space you need to keep warm,
while avoiding the hidden risks of improvised heating.

> **Beware carbon monoxide.** Never run a generator, barbecue, camping stove, or
> fuel heater in a closed room — the invisible fumes kill. Use only heaters meant
> for indoors, with ventilation, and keep flames away from bedding.

## Trap your body heat

- **Layer up:** several thin layers trap more warmth than one thick one. Keep a
  dry inner layer; wet clothing chills you fast.
- **Cover the extremes:** much heat is lost from the head, neck, hands, and feet —
  hat, scarf, gloves, warm socks.
- **Keep moving gently** to generate heat, but don't sweat — damp clothes steal
  warmth.
- **Eat and drink warm** if you can; food fuels your internal furnace.

## Warm one room, not the home

1. **Choose a small room** with few windows and gather everyone into it — shared
   body heat helps.
2. **Seal draughts:** block gaps under doors and around windows with cloth or
   tape; cover windows with blankets or bubble wrap at night.
3. **Insulate yourself from the floor and walls** — sit and sleep on a mattress,
   cushions, or thick bedding, not bare cold surfaces.
4. **Make a warm sleeping nest** — a small tent or blanket fort inside the room
   traps heat around you.

## Avoid frostbite and hypothermia

- **Frostbite** (usually fingers, toes, ears, nose): skin turns white/grey, numb,
  then hard. Warm the area gently with body heat or warm (not hot) water; **don't
  rub** it; get medical help.
- **Hypothermia:** shivering, slurred speech, clumsiness, confusion, drowsiness.
  This is an **emergency**: move the person somewhere warm, replace wet clothes
  with dry, wrap them (including head), give warm sweet drinks if alert, and get
  help. Handle them gently.

## Look out for each other

Older people, babies, and the unwell lose heat fastest and may not feel the cold —
check on them and on neighbours often, and bring people together where it's
warmest.

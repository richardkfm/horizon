# Stay safe in a wildfire

Wildfires move fast — far faster than people expect, especially uphill and in
wind — and the smoke can harm people for miles around. The two things that save
lives are **preparing your surroundings** in advance and **leaving early** when
fire threatens.

> **When in doubt, get out — early.** Don't wait to see flames. Late evacuation,
> on roads choked with smoke and traffic, is when people die. If officials say go,
> go immediately; if you feel unsafe, leave even before they do.

## Prepare before the season

- **Defensible space:** clear dry vegetation, dead leaves, and flammable clutter
  away from the building; keep grass short and gutters clear; move woodpiles and
  fuel away from walls.
- **Harden the home** where you can: cover vents with fine mesh (embers get in
  through gaps), use non-flammable roofing, and keep tools and water to hand.
- **Plan and pack:** know **at least two escape routes**, keep a go-bag ready, and
  agree a meeting point and an out-of-area contact.
- **Stay informed:** know the local warnings and have a battery radio.

## If fire threatens

1. **Leave early** by your planned route, taking your go-bag, wearing
   long cotton/wool clothing and sturdy shoes (not synthetics, which melt).
2. **As you go** (only if there's time and it's safe): shut windows and doors,
   move flammable furniture from windows, and leave a light on so the house is
   visible in smoke.
3. **Don't drive into smoke or flames;** if the road is blocked, turn back.

## Cope with smoke

Smoke is a danger far beyond the flames, especially for children, the elderly, and
people with heart or lung conditions:

- **Stay indoors with windows shut;** make one room a cleaner-air space and reduce
  indoor smoke and dust.
- **Reduce exertion** when air is bad; a well-fitting mask helps outdoors.
- **Watch the vulnerable** and move them to cleaner air if you can.

## If you cannot escape

- **Avoid the path of the fire:** it runs **fastest uphill and in the wind**, so
  steer away from slopes and gullies above the fire.
- Seek the **least vegetated ground** — a wide cleared area, a large rock or dirt
  patch, or (last resort) a building — and shelter from radiant heat.
- **Protect your airway** from heat and smoke and stay low.

## Urban vs out of town

- **At the city/wildland edge:** the main threat is **ember attack** igniting
  homes — defensible space and sealed vents matter most, and smoke blankets whole
  cities.
- **Out of town:** fire spreads faster with more fuel and you may be **cut off**;
  leave earlier, keep vehicles fuelled and pointed out, protect water sources and
  **move livestock** to grazed or cleared ground early.

## After the fire

Return only when cleared. Watch for **hotspots, smouldering ground, and falling
trees**, weakened structures, and ongoing **poor air quality**.

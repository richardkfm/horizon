# Build a slow sand filter

A slow sand filter is a simple, low-tech way to make surface water much safer to
drink. Water trickles slowly through fine sand where a living biological layer
(the *schmutzdecke*) removes most pathogens.

> **Risk:** A slow sand filter reduces but does not guarantee removal of all
> contaminants. Always pair it with [water testing](#) and, where possible, a
> final disinfection step (boiling, chlorine, or solar). Never assume output is
> safe without testing.

## Materials

- A clean food-grade barrel or tank (≈ 60–200 L)
- Fine, washed sand (the main filter bed)
- Coarse gravel (support layer)
- A perforated outlet pipe and tap
- A diffuser plate (to avoid disturbing the sand surface)

## Procedure

1. **Wash the sand and gravel** thoroughly until rinse water runs clear.
2. **Layer the bed:** gravel at the bottom around the outlet pipe, then a thick
   layer (≥ 60 cm) of fine sand on top.
3. **Add a diffuser** above the sand so incoming water does not dig a hole.
4. **Charge the filter:** keep a few centimetres of water standing above the sand
   at all times so the biological layer stays alive.
5. **Ripen the filter:** run it continuously for 1–3 weeks so the schmutzdecke
   develops before relying on the output.
6. **Run slowly:** aim for a slow, steady flow. Faster flow means worse filtering.

## Storage & distribution

- Store filtered water in clean, covered containers.
- Use a tap or ladle to avoid recontamination by hands.
- Test the output periodically.

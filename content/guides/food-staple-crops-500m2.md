# Grow staple crops on 500 m²

A 500 m² plot, well used, can provide a meaningful share of a small group's
calories. The goal here is *calories and storability*, not variety — focus on a
few reliable staples suited to your climate.

> **Risk:** Soil contamination (heavy metals, sewage) makes food unsafe. If you
> are unsure of a site's history, test the soil before growing food on it.

## Materials

- Seeds or tubers of locally-proven staples (e.g. potatoes, beans, maize, squash)
- Basic hand tools (fork, hoe, rake)
- Compost or other organic matter
- Mulch material

## Procedure

1. **Assess the site:** sunlight, drainage, and soil. Aim for 6+ hours of sun.
2. **Build the soil:** add compost and organic matter; avoid leaving soil bare.
3. **Choose staples for your climate** and stagger planting to spread the harvest.
4. **Plant at the right depth and spacing** for each crop.
5. **Mulch** to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
6. **Water deeply but less often** to encourage strong roots.
7. **Harvest and cure** for storage (e.g. cure potatoes and squash before storing).

## Seed saving

- Let a few of your strongest plants go to seed.
- Dry seeds fully and store them cool, dark, and dry, labelled with the date.

# Make simple instruments and keep a rhythm

Music binds people together, lifts spirits during hard work, and marks births,
harvests, and farewells. You need no electricity and no bought instruments — just
materials at hand and a willingness to begin.

> **Welcoming over polished:** The aim is for everyone to join in, not to sound
> perfect. A group keeping one happy beat beats a flawless solo no one shares.

## Make instruments from found things

- **Shakers:** seal dried seeds, beans, or small stones in a tin, gourd, or
  tightly tied cloth pouch.
- **Drums:** stretch a damp hide, thick cloth, or a salvaged sheet over an open
  pot, bucket, or hollow log and lash it tight; it tightens as it dries.
- **Clappers and sticks:** two hardwood sticks, or two stones, struck together.
- **Flutes and whistles:** cut notches or holes in a hollow reed or bamboo; move
  fingers over the holes to change pitch.
- **Plucked string:** stretch cord or gut over a springy bow or a box; tighter
  strings sound higher.

## Keep a steady beat

1. Choose a **pulse** everyone can feel — like a calm walking pace or a heartbeat.
2. Start it with your body first: clap, stamp, or pat your knees together.
3. Count groups of four ("1-2-3-4") so people know where the beat returns.
4. Add a simple repeating pattern on top once the pulse is solid.

## Lead a group

- Start simple and slow; speed up only once everyone is together.
- Use **call and response:** you play a short pattern, the group echoes it.
- Give people without instruments a part — clapping, stamping, or singing.
- Watch the group and match their energy; the leader serves the circle, not the
  other way around.

# Protect your household during a disease outbreak

In an epidemic or pandemic, simple, consistent habits protect you and slow the
spread far more than panic does. The basics — distance, hygiene, clean air, and
careful care of the sick — work against most infectious diseases.

> **Follow trusted health guidance.** Outbreaks differ in how they spread and what
> stops them. Get instructions from credible health authorities and adapt these
> general principles to the specific illness.

## Reduce your exposure

- **Keep distance** from people outside your household when an illness is
  spreading, especially indoors and in crowds.
- **Ventilate:** fresh air dilutes airborne germs. Open windows, meet outdoors
  where you can, and avoid stuffy, packed spaces.
- **Cover coughs and sneezes**, and wear a well-fitting mask where authorities
  advise, particularly indoors with others or when caring for the sick.
- **Limit unnecessary trips and contacts** during peak spread.

## Hygiene basics

- **Wash hands** often with soap and water for a good 20 seconds — after going out,
  before eating, and after contact with the sick. Use sanitiser when you can't
  wash.
- **Avoid touching your face** with unwashed hands.
- **Clean shared surfaces** (handles, taps, phones) regularly.
- **Keep clean water and sanitation going** — many outbreaks spread through water
  and poor hygiene (see the water guides).

## Prepare your household

- **Stock essentials** for a period at home: food, water, soap, medicines,
  fever/pain relief, and any regular prescriptions.
- **Plan for illness:** decide who would care for the sick, and set aside a room
  or corner where someone unwell can rest apart from others.
- **Keep a thermometer and basic first-aid supplies**, and know the warning signs
  that mean you should seek medical help.

## Care for the sick safely

1. **Isolate the unwell person** in a separate, ventilated room if possible; limit
   who goes in.
2. **One carer where you can**, ideally not someone at high risk; carer and patient
   wear masks during contact.
3. **Hydration and rest** are the foundation; treat fever and discomfort as
   advised.
4. **Hygiene around care:** wash hands before and after, don't share cups or
   towels, and clean surfaces and laundry the patient uses.
5. **Watch for danger signs** — trouble breathing, persistent high fever,
   confusion, blue lips, or a fast decline — and get medical help promptly.

## Look after the community

Check on neighbours who live alone or are at high risk, share accurate
information, and resist rumours and stigma — caring for each other, calmly and
fairly, is what gets a community through an outbreak.

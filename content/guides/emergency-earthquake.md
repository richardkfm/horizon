# Stay safe in an earthquake

Earthquakes give no warning and are over in seconds — so what you do *during* the
shaking, by reflex, matters most. Most injuries come from falling objects and
broken glass, not collapsing buildings, so the goal is to protect yourself where
you are.

> **Drop, Cover, Hold On.** Drop to your hands and knees, take cover under a sturdy
> table (or against an interior wall, protecting your head and neck), and hold on
> until the shaking stops. **Do not run outside during the shaking** — that's when
> falling debris hurts people.

## During the shaking

- **Indoors:** Drop, Cover, Hold On. Stay away from windows, mirrors, and tall
  furniture. If in bed, stay there and protect your head with a pillow.
- **Outdoors:** move to an **open area** away from buildings, walls, trees, and
  power lines, then drop down.
- **Driving:** pull over away from bridges, overpasses, and power lines; stop and
  stay inside until it ends.
- **Do not use lifts**, and don't rush for doorways — they're no safer than under
  sturdy cover.

## After the shaking stops

1. **Expect aftershocks** — they can be strong and bring down already-weakened
   structures. Drop, Cover, Hold On again each time.
2. **Check yourself and others** for injuries and give first aid.
3. **Check for hazards:** if you **smell gas**, shut it off, open windows, and get
   out — **no flames, no light switches, no electrics**. Watch for fire, water
   leaks, and damaged wiring.
4. **Leave a damaged building carefully** once shaking stops, watching for falling
   debris, and don't re-enter until it's confirmed safe.

## Urban vs out of town

- **In town:** in a high-rise, stay in and take cover — expect alarms and
  sprinklers, and **never use lifts**. Beware **falling glass and façade pieces**
  around tall buildings. Roads, water, and power may be down for days; keep
  supplies.
- **Out of town:** the ground itself is the danger — watch for **landslides and
  rockfall** on slopes, ground cracks, and **dam or bridge failure** downstream.
  Help may be far off, so be ready to be self-sufficient and to signal.
- **Near the coast:** strong or long shaking is a natural **tsunami warning** —
  move to high ground immediately and don't wait for an official alert.

## Prepare beforehand

Secure tall furniture and heavy items to walls, keep shoes and a torch by the bed
(broken glass), know how to shut off gas/water/power, and keep a go-bag and a few
days of water and food.

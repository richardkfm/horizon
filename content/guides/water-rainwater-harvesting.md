# Harvest and store rainwater

Rain falling on a roof is one of the easiest water sources to capture — no
pump, no permission from a river or well, and it renews with every storm. A
simple catchment system turns a roof you already have into a standing water
supply.

> **Risk:** Roof-caught rainwater collects whatever sits on the roof — dust,
> bird droppings, leaves, and residue from the roofing material itself. Treat
> it like any untested source: settle, filter, and disinfect before drinking
> (see which water treatment should you use).

## Choose a clean catchment surface

- **Best:** metal, tile, or untreated, food-safe roofing.
- **Avoid or treat with caution:** roofs with lead flashing, asbestos
  sheeting, or old chemical treatments — don't drink runoff from these
  without knowing what's in them; use it for non-drinking purposes instead
  (irrigation, washing).
- **Keep gutters clear** of leaves and debris so water actually reaches the
  tank instead of overflowing or stagnating.

## Divert the first flush

The first few minutes of a storm wash the most dust, droppings, and debris
off the roof — let that go to waste, not into your tank.

```ascii
   roof
    |
    v
  gutter ---> downpipe
                 |
                 v
         [ first-flush diverter ]  <- fills with the dirty first litres,
                 |                     then closes off automatically
                 v
             storage tank  <---- clean water only, once the diverter is full
```

*Fig. 1: a first-flush diverter — a capped length of pipe below the downpipe
fills with the dirtiest initial runoff, then a float or valve routes the
cleaner water that follows on into the tank*

- A simple diverter is just a capped pipe branching off the downpipe before
  the tank inlet — size it for roughly the first litre per square metre of
  roof.
- **Empty and clean the diverter** after each significant rain so it's ready
  to work again.
- Fit a **coarse screen or leaf guard** at the tank inlet too, to keep out
  debris and to discourage mosquitoes from breeding in the tank.

## Size your storage

Estimate how much you can realistically collect:

> **Spec:** collected litres ≈ roof area (m²) × rainfall (mm) × 0.8 (losses
> to overflow, evaporation, and the first flush). A 50 m² roof in 20 mm of
> rain yields roughly **800 litres** per storm.

- Compare this to your household's need (see the water store checklist for a
  target — 3+ litres per person per day) to judge how many storms' worth of
  tank capacity you actually need.
- For sizing the tank itself against your space and budget, see estimate
  material quantities and measure area and volume in the calculations guides.

## Store it safely

- Use **food-grade, opaque or shaded containers** — light encourages algae
  growth inside the tank.
- **Keep the tank covered** at all times except for the screened inlet and
  an overflow outlet.
- **Draw from a tap near the bottom** but slightly raised, so sediment
  settles below the draw point rather than being stirred into what you use.

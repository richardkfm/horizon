# Stop severe bleeding

Heavy bleeding can become life-threatening in minutes, so acting fast matters more
than acting perfectly. The core idea is simple: **press hard, right on the wound,
and keep pressing.**

> **Do now:** Put firm, direct pressure on the wound with whatever clean cloth you
> have and press hard without letting up. Get the person to professional care.

> **Warning:** This is first aid, not a substitute for a doctor or hospital. Get
> professional medical help as fast as you can. If you have no training and there
> is any way to reach emergency services, do so.

## Press to stop the flow

1. **Protect yourself** if you can — use gloves or a plastic bag over your hand to
   avoid contact with blood.
2. **Press directly on the wound** with a clean cloth, gauze, or your hand. Use
   your whole body weight if needed.
3. **Do not keep peeking.** Lifting the cloth to look restarts the bleeding. If
   blood soaks through, add more cloth on top and keep pressing — don't remove the
   first layer.

4. **Lay the person down** and raise the wounded part above the heart if no bone
   looks broken.

## Pack a deep wound

For a deep wound in the limb, neck, or groin where pressure alone is not enough:

- **Pack it tightly** with clean gauze or cloth, pushing it firmly into the wound
  to fill the space, then keep hard pressure on top.

## Tourniquet — only for life-threatening limb bleeding

If an arm or leg is bleeding so badly it won't stop and the person could die:

1. **Place a tourniquet** (a wide band, never wire or string) 5–7 cm above the
   wound, not over a joint.
2. **Tighten until the bleeding stops** — it will hurt; that is expected.
3. **Note the time** it went on and tell the medics. Do not loosen it once
   applied; only a clinician should remove it.

## After bleeding is controlled

- **Keep the person warm** and still, and watch for shock (pale, cold, clammy,
  fast breathing) — see the *fractures and sprains* and first-aid guides.
- **Keep pressure or the dressing in place** until professional help takes over.

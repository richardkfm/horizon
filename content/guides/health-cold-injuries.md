# Treat hypothermia and frostbite

Cold injures the body in two ways: **hypothermia** (the whole body cools too far)
and **frostbite** (skin and tissue freeze). Both creep up quietly, so watch for the
early signs and act before they get serious.

> **Warning:** First aid only. Severe hypothermia and deep frostbite are medical
> emergencies — get the person to professional care. Warm gently; rough handling or
> fast re-warming can be dangerous.

## Hypothermia — the body is too cold

**Signs**, in rough order as it worsens: shivering, cold and pale skin, clumsiness,
slurred speech, confusion or drowsiness, then shivering *stopping* (a bad sign).

1. **Get them out of the cold** — indoors, or sheltered from wind and wet.
2. **Remove wet clothing** and replace with dry layers; cover the head.
3. **Insulate from the ground** and wrap them in blankets, a sleeping bag, or dry
   coats. Share body heat if needed.
4. **Give warm, sweet drinks** if they are fully awake and able to swallow — never
   alcohol.
5. **Warm gently and gradually.** Don't use direct high heat (hot water bottles
   straight on skin, a fire too close) and don't rub the limbs.
6. **Handle a very cold person gently** and keep them still — rough movement can
   trigger dangerous heart rhythms. Get urgent help.

## Frostbite — frozen skin

**Signs:** cold, hard, pale or waxy skin that may feel numb; often fingers, toes,
ears, nose, or cheeks. As it thaws it becomes red, painful, and may blister.

1. **Get out of the cold** and treat any hypothermia first — that comes before the
   frostbite.
2. **Don't rub** the area or warm it with direct high heat.
3. **Re-warm gently** by soaking in warm (not hot) water, around body temperature,
   or with skin-to-skin contact — only once you're sure it won't re-freeze.
   Re-freezing thawed tissue causes far worse damage.
4. **Protect the area:** separate frostbitten fingers/toes with dry dressings,
   don't burst blisters, and avoid walking on frostbitten feet if you can help it.

## Get urgent help if

Confusion or drowsiness sets in, shivering stops in a cold person, the skin stays
hard/white after re-warming, or large areas are affected. Keep the person warm,
dry, and still while you arrange care. See also the *survive extreme cold* guide.
